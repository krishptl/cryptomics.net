SOLUTION

01

Store genomes like documents

Ingest genomic files and attach them to a durable, document-like record that can be referenced, archived, and shared without exposing the underlying raw data by default.

02

Lock everything in an FHE vault

Keep genomic data encrypted at rest and in use using fully homomorphic encryption (FHE) where applicable, so analysis can begin without first decrypting entire genomes.

03

Share via signed access grants

Use Cryptomics like a genomic Parchment or DocuSign: send structured access requests, capture signatures and approvals, and create a clear record of who is allowed to use which datasets and for what purpose.

04

Audit every request

Generate an auditable trail of access grants, signatures, and data flows so institutions can show exactly how genomic data was handled across research, clinical, and operational workflows.

WHY CRYPTOMICS

A vault, a signing layer, and a roadmap to fine-grained genomic control.

FHE-secured genomic vault

  • Genomic data is stored encrypted by default, with fully homomorphic encryption (FHE) workflows for sensitive computation.
  • Modern storage encryption (e.g., AES-256) and key management to protect genomes at rest.
  • A foundation designed to support more granular, nucleotide-aware encryption in future iterations.

Parchment-style genomic records

  • Treat genomes like high-stakes documents, with clear ownership, retention, and sharing rules.
  • Structured records that can be referenced in contracts, protocols, and institutional workflows.
  • Built to integrate with lab, research, and hospital systems instead of replacing them.

DocuSign-style consent & auditability

  • Capture signatures, approvals, and consent decisions around genomic data access.
  • Maintain a tamper-evident record of who requested, approved, and used which datasets.
  • Designed to support HIPAA/GDPR-aligned workflows and institutional governance processes.
